Welcome to simple cooking

Here you will find recipes that you can easily and quickly prepare in 30 minutes. Since we don't have much time to cook and shop, here are some tips on how to easily reduce waste.

Zero Waste

Every year, the average European throws away 16 tons of rubbish. As only 40% of waste is recyclable, landfills are getting bigger and more polluting our planet. From wasted food to plastic packaging, the kitchen is the biggest source of rubbish in our home. Check out some simple ways to reduce the amount of waste generated at the heart of every household.

  • Say NO to disposable foil packaging
    First, bring cotton (or reusable) bags for shopping. These plastic bags can take up to 1,000 years to decompose!
    Invest in special fruit and vegetable bags / nets. Thanks to this, you will avoid plastic packaging from the supermarket.
  • Be smart about recycling
    Unfortunately, the recycling of plastics reduces the quality of the material, which means that they are not transformed into new containers, but into cheap plastic bags that eventually end up in a landfill. If possible, use glass or metal packaging as they can be recycled without any loss in quality.
    Try to give things a new life! Jam jars are perfect for spices or pasta, you can make your own home-made olive oil in glass bottles.
  • Learn how to throw away less food
    From freezing vegetables and fruits to drying meat, there are many ways to avoid food waste. The freezer is your new weapon! Uneaten vegetables or fruit can be frozen whole or blended, they will be perfect for soups and cocktails.
    Place products with shorter expiry dates closer to each other to prevent them from expiring.
    Has the expiry date passed? No worries! Most products are valid for up to 4 days after their expiry date. If the smell and texture have not changed, you can safely use the product.
  • Buy less and locally if possible
    Supermarkets encourage very large purchases, the most common cause of food waste. Find local grocery stores and visit them regularly to buy fresh produce such as fruit, vegetables, bread and meat. Also, give slightly bruised or imperfect fruit and vegetables a chance - unfortunately, most of them end up in the basket.
    Since keeping animals such as cows, pigs and chickens has a huge impact on environmental pollution, try to limit meat products in your diet. Choose recipes that use whole meat to keep it as little as possible in the basket.
  • Creatively use leftovers
    Did you know you can use orange peel to keep mosquitoes away? And can you easily turn potato peelings into chips? If you want your kitchen to be more zero-waste, check what you can do with the leftovers after each cooking. The Internet can surprise you pleasantly

On our website, in the recipes tab, you will find some tips and solutions on what to do with leftovers.
